Rhinos - The Majestic Creatures

Learn all about the mighty rhino and its incredible world!

About Rhinos

Rhinos are large, powerful herbivores known for their thick skin and impressive horns. Found mostly in Africa and Asia, these incredible animals are a sight to behold. Despite their size and strength, rhinos are gentle giants that mostly feed on grasses.

Species of Rhinos

There are five species of rhinos: White, Black, Indian, Javan, and Sumatran. Each species has unique characteristics and habitats. Unfortunately, rhinos are critically endangered due to poaching and habitat loss, and conservation efforts are crucial for their survival.

Fun Facts About Rhinos

  • Rhino horns are made of keratin, the same protein found in human hair and nails.
  • They have poor eyesight but an excellent sense of smell and hearing.
  • Rhinos can weigh as much as a small car but can run up to 30 miles per hour!
